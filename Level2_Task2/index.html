About Bill Gates

William Henry Gates III, born on October 28, 1955, is an American business magnate, software developer, investor, and philanthropist. He is best known as the co-founder of Microsoft, the world's largest personal-computer software company. Gates led the company as chairman and CEO until stepping down in 2000 to focus on charitable work through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

Throughout his life, Gates has played a pivotal role in the technological revolution, bringing personal computing to the masses. With his philanthropy, he has also made significant contributions to healthcare, education, and poverty eradication globally.

Legacy and Achievements

  • Founded Microsoft in 1975 with Paul Allen.
  • Developed Windows, a widely used operating system.
  • Consistently ranked among the world's wealthiest people.
  • Co-founded the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.
  • Supports global health and education through philanthropy.
